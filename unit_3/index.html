Love Letter to Paris

Ideas for couples visiting the city of light


Hotel de la ville Marvel at the beauty of this iconic building and have an excuse to recreate the classic kiss photo by Robert Doisneau in black and white. Magnum photographers.


Luxembourg Gardens Pick up some macaroons from DALLOYAU and head to the park for a leisurely stroll or decadent hangout in these beautiful gardens. In the summertime there are free concerts. Macaroons.


Backstreets of Montmartre Although the Sacre Coeur and Place du Tertre are the most known tourist areas, walking off across the back and sides streets of Montmartre are the most charming and tranquil. Art history.


Bridge strolling Paris is a city of bridges. They have many different personalities. Among the more notable, Pont Alexander III, Pont Neuf, et Pont des Arts. Sit close and enjoy the sunset or the sunrise at one of these viewpoints


Ile st Louis This is a more intimate and old world version of Ile de la Cite. Wander the narrow streets and imagine you have returned to ages past. In the summertime grab an icecream at the infamous BERTHILLON, or for wintertime warming, grab a hot chocolate at one of the tiny nook in the wall brasseries. Berthillon.


Square du Vert Galant If you and your beloved have tired feet, this is a stunning resting place at the tip of Ile de la Cite. This place, as well as most of the banks of the Seine, are spots for the lovers to lie down and kiss, take a nap, or discuss French philosophy. Well, mostly the former! French Philosphy


Early morning Boulangerie et Patisserie Wake up early so you can get to these french institutions and get high on the smell of freshly baked delicious things. Share a "baguette", a "pain au chocolat", a "pepites au chocolat", or other offerings.


Train Bleu, Gare du Lyon If you want to pretend you are living out the real life episode of a French film noir, come and take time at this glorious looking restaurant nestled inside a railway station. You may end up spontaneously taking a train to somewhere. If trains are not your thing, other historic beautiful suggestions for dinner are Closerie des Lilas, La Procope, Maxims, La Coupole et les Deux Magots


A day trip to Versailles. Play King and Queen for the day in this drop dead gorgeous palace


OK so you want the Eiffel Tower? One of the most iconic, and less frenetic, views is from the Trocadero. It also makes for a good dance floor if you get really carried away. Trust me, I have seen it happen!
